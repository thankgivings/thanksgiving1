Located in downtown Dallas, this plaza is the vision of developer Peter Stewart who envisioned a public place for ecumenical worship to reflect on the world’s blessings and commemorate the nation’s long observance of Thanksgiving. Peter Stewart, a real-estate developer, in the early 1960s was a member of a commission planning to revitalize downtown Dallas as freeways slashed through it. In 1968 a booklet of Dallasites’ reflections on the theme of Thanksgiving was edited by Peter Stewart and published with the title The Spirit of Thanksgiving—Testimony of the People. The magnificent Glory Window in the Chapel at Thanks-Giving Square consists of seventy-three stained-glass panels.
